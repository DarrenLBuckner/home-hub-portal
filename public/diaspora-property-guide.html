üåç Can Foreigners and Diaspora Buy Property in Guyana?

Yes! Guyana's constitution protects foreign property ownership, making it one of the most welcoming countries in the Caribbean for diaspora investment.

Key Restrictions to Know:

  • Border Proximity: Land within 40 miles of international borders requires special government permission
  • Large Purchases: Properties over 3 acres require an Alien Landholding License
  • Standard Properties: Most residential and commercial properties have no restrictions

üìã Step-by-Step Buying Process

1

Property Search & Selection

  • Work with licensed real estate agents
  • Research locations thoroughly
  • Consider proximity to amenities and transportation
2

Legal Representation

  • Hire a qualified Guyanese attorney
  • Verify their credentials with the Guyana Bar Association
  • Ensure they specialize in property law
3

Due Diligence

  • Verify property title at the Deeds Registry
  • Check for any liens or encumbrances
  • Confirm boundaries and measurements
  • Review any existing tenancies
4

Agreement of Sale

  • Negotiate terms and conditions
  • Sign legally binding purchase agreement
  • Pay initial deposit (typically 10-20%)
5

Transfer and Completion

  • Complete official property transfer
  • Pay remaining balance
  • Register transfer at Deeds Registry
  • Receive your property documents

Typical Costs (as percentage of property value):

  • Legal Fees: 1-2% of property value
  • Stamp Duty: Approximately 2% of property value
  • Registration Fees: Varies by property value
  • Property Tax: Annual ongoing cost
  • Survey Fees: If required

üè¶ Financing Options

1. Cash Purchase (Most Common)

Simplest and fastest method. Ensures clear ownership without mortgage complications.

2. Home Country Financing

Some international banks offer loans for Caribbean property purchases. Check with your bank about overseas property loans.

3. Limited Local Financing

Few Guyanese banks offer mortgages to non-residents. Typically requires substantial local presence.

4. Seller Financing

Rare but occasionally available for unique properties or motivated sellers.

üö® Fraud Prevention Essentials

Red Flags to Watch For:

  • Deals that seem "too good to be true"
  • Pressure to send money quickly
  • Sellers who refuse to meet in person or via video call
  • Properties offered significantly below market value
  • Requests for payment to personal accounts

Protection Strategies:

  • Always use a licensed attorney for all transactions
  • Verify everything independently through official sources
  • Use secure payment channels with proper documentation
  • Visit the property in person or have a trusted representative inspect it
  • Check agent credentials with the Real Estate Board
